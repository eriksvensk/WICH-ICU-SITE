About the Study

Background & Rationale

Unnecessary diagnostic testing in critical care can cause patient discomfort, waste resources, and does not always improve outcomes. WICH-ICU evaluates a pragmatic package of Choosing Wisely recommendations to reduce low-value routine testing in Swedish ICUs.

Design

Registry-based stepped-wedge cluster randomized controlled trial. Adult ICU patients (≥18 years) in participating Swedish ICUs are included through the Swedish Intensive Care Registry (SIR). Each ICU transitions from control to intervention according to a randomized schedule.

Outcomes

  • Primary: 30-day mortality (non-inferiority)
  • Secondary: ICU length of stay; duration of invasive/non-invasive ventilation; CRRT duration; ICU readmission within 30 days
  • Process: ABG count per patient-day; venous lab tests per patient-day; chest X-rays per patient-day

Monitoring & Data

Local clinical monitors perform 5-day counts of arterial blood gases, venous labs, and chest radiographs at baseline and twice post-implementation. Outcome and case-mix data are obtained from SIR (coverage >95% of Swedish ICU beds).

Why it matters

If safe, reducing low-value tests means less patient burden, better resource stewardship, and potential environmental benefits from fewer consumables.

Trial registration: NCT07013175

    Choosing Wisely in ICU – Intervention Package

    Arterial blood gases (ABG)

    • Use ABGs for clinical deterioration, treatment changes, or protocolized needs (e.g., CRRT, DKA, insulin).
    • Avoid routine ABGs in stable patients or at fixed times/for rounds.

    Venous laboratory testing

    • Avoid routine “daily lab panels” without indication.
    • Base testing on clinical need; do not repeat fresh admission labs unnecessarily.
    • Ensure required data for scoring (e.g., SAPS3) and research are obtained.

    Chest radiography

    • No routine daily CXRs for ventilated patients.
    • Order CXR only when results will change management (e.g., suspected pneumothorax, selected line checks, acute deterioration).

    Each ICU adapts implementation locally with education, reminders at order entry, and follow-up by the local monitor.
